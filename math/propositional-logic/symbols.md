# Symbols

| Symbol | Meaning | Example                       |
|--------|---------|-------------------------------|
| $\neg$ | Negation| $\neg p$ is the inverse of $p$|
| $\land$| Logical AND | $p \land q$ is eq to $p$ `AND` $q$|
| $\lor$ | Logical OR | $p \lor q$ is eq to $p$ `OR` $q$|
| $\oplus$| XOR | $p \oplus q$ is eq to $p$ `XOR` $q$|
| $\to$ | Implication | $p \to q$ means $p$ implies $q$|
| $\iff$ | Bi-implication | $p \iff q$ means $p$ is only true if $q$ is true|
| $\equiv$ | Logical equivalence | Basically, equals $p \lor \mathbf{T}$ $\equiv$ $\mathbf{T}$|
| $\mathbf{T}$| True | True |
| $\mathbf{F}$| False | False |
| $P(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$ | Predicate with $n$ variables ||
| $\forall x P(x)$ | Universal quantifier (for all x in the predicate $P(x)$) |
| $\exists x P(x)$ | There exists $x$ in $P(x)$ such that...||
| $\vdots$ | Vertical Dots ||
-----------------------------
